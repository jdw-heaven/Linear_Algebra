我们先介绍一下“Imaginary Time-evolving Block Decimation”（ITEBD）。

就我理解，这是一种基于“power method”的一种求解矩阵最小本征值（也就是基态）的一种方法。通过“虚时演化+归一化“的方式将系统的状态还原到基态。

下面我们将具体来讲述该算法的基本原理：

1. 波函数的含时演化

在薛定谔绘景下，t 系统时刻的状态波函数可以表示为（初始时刻记为时间零点）：
∣Ψt⟩=exp⁡(−iHt)∣Ψ0⟩\ket{\Psi_t} = \exp(-i Ht)\ket{\Psi_0}∣Ψt​⟩=exp(−iHt)∣Ψ0​⟩
虚时情况下：
∣Ψτ⟩=exp⁡(−Hτ)∣Ψ0⟩∥exp⁡(−Hτ)∣Ψ0⟩∥\ket{\Psi_\tau} = \frac{\exp(-H\tau)\ket{\Psi_0}}{\|\exp(-H\tau)\ket{\Psi_0}\|}∣Ψτ​⟩=∥exp(−Hτ)∣Ψ0​⟩∥exp(−Hτ)∣Ψ0​⟩​
如果了解“power method”，那么应该可以看出虚时情况下的公式就是我们用来求解矩阵最小本征值的式子。

3. Matrix Product State

基于奇异值分解我们可以将系统的状态用多个矩阵乘积的形式表示[1]：

SVD guarantees for an arbitrary (rectangular) matrix MMM of dimensions (NA×NB)(N_A \times N_B)(NA​×NB​) the existence of a decomposition:

M=USV†,M = U S V^\dagger,M=USV†,

where

  • UUU is of dimension (NA×min⁡(NA,NB))(N_A \times \min (N_A,N_B))(NA​×min(NA​,NB​)) and has orthonormal columns (the left singular vectors), i.e. U†U=IU^\dagger U = IU†U=I; if NA≤NBN_A \leq N_BNA​≤NB​ this implies that it is unitary, and also UU†=IUU^\dagger = IUU†=I.
  • SSS is of dimension (min⁡(NA,NB)×min⁡(NA,NB))(\min (N_A,N_B) \times \min (N_A,N_B))(min(NA​,NB​)×min(NA​,NB​)), diagonal with non-negative entries Saa≡saS_{aa} \equiv s_aSaa​≡sa​. These are the so-called singular values. The number rrr of non-zero singular values is the (Schmidt) rank of MMM. In the following, we assume descending order: s1≥s2≥…≥sr>0s_1 \geq s_2 \geq \ldots \geq s_r > 0s1​≥s2​≥…≥sr​>0.
  • V†V^\daggerV† is of dimension (min⁡(NA,NB)×NB)(\min (N_A,N_B) \times N_B)(min(NA​,NB​)×NB​) and has orthonormal rows (the right singular vectors), i.e. V†V=IV^\dagger V = IV†V=I. If NA≥NBN_A \geq N_BNA​≥NB​ this implies that it is unitary, and also VV†=IVV^\dagger = IVV†=I.

Consider a lattice of LLL sites with ddd-dimensional local state spaces {σi}\{ \sigma_i \}{σi​} on sites i=1,…,Li=1,\ldots,Li=1,…,L. In fact, while we will be naturally thinking of a one-dimensional lattice, the following also holds for a lattice of arbitrary dimension on which sites have been numbered; however, MPS generated from states on higher-dimensional lattices will not be manageable in numerical practice. The most general pure quantum state on the lattice reads

∣ψ⟩=∑σ1,…,σLcσ1…σL∣σ1,…,σL⟩,\ket{\psi} = \sum_{\sigma_1,\ldots,\sigma_L} c_{\sigma_1 \ldots \sigma_L} \ket{\sigma_1,\ldots,\sigma_L},∣ψ⟩=σ1​,…,σL​∑​cσ1​…σL​​∣σ1​,…,σL​⟩,

where we have exponentially many coefficients cσ1…σLc_{\sigma_1 \ldots \sigma_L}cσ1​…σL​​ with quite oblique content in typical quantum many-body problems. Let us assume that it is normalized. Can we find a notation that gives a more local notion of the state (while preserving the quantum non-locality of the state)? Indeed, SVD allows us to do just that. The result may look quite forbidding, but will be shown to relate profoundly to familiar concepts of quantum physics. There are three ways of doing this that are of relevance to us.

Left-canonical matrix product state. In a first step, we reshape the state vector with dLd^LdL components into a matrix Ψ\PsiΨ of dimension (d×dL−1)(d \times d^{L-1})(d×dL−1), where the coefficients are related as

Ψσ1,(σ2…σL)=cσ1…σL.\Psi_{\sigma_1, (\sigma_2 \ldots \sigma_L)} = c_{\sigma_1 \ldots \sigma_L} .Ψσ1​,(σ2​…σL​)​=cσ1​…σL​​.

An SVD of Ψ\PsiΨ gives

cσ1…σL=Ψσ1,(σ2…σL)=∑a1r1Uσ1,a1Sa1,a1(V†)a1,(σ2…σL)≡∑a1r1Uσ1,a1ca1σ2…σL,c_{\sigma_1 \ldots \sigma_L} = \Psi_{\sigma_1, (\sigma_2 \ldots \sigma_L)} = \sum_{a_1}^{r_1} U_{\sigma_1,a_1} S_{a_1,a_1} (V^\dagger)_{a_1, (\sigma_2 \ldots \sigma_L)} \equiv \sum_{a_1}^{r_1} U_{\sigma_1,a_1} c_{a_1\sigma_2\ldots\sigma_L} ,cσ1​…σL​​=Ψσ1​,(σ2​…σL​)​=a1​∑r1​​Uσ1​,a1​​Sa1​,a1​​(V†)a1​,(σ2​…σL​)​≡a1​∑r1​​Uσ1​,a1​​ca1​σ2​…σL​​,

where in the last equality SSS and V†V^\daggerV† have been multiplied and the resulting matrix has been reshaped back into a vector. The rank is r1≤dr_1 \leq dr1​≤d. We now decompose the matrix UUU into a collection of ddd row vectors Aσ1A^{\sigma_1}Aσ1​ with entries Aa1σ1=Uσ1,a1A^{\sigma_1}_{a_1}=U_{\sigma_1,a_1}Aa1​σ1​​=Uσ1​,a1​​. At the same time, we reshape ca1σ2…σLc_{a_1\sigma_2\ldots\sigma_L}ca1​σ2​…σL​​ into a matrix Ψ(a1σ2),(σ3…σL)\Psi_{(a_1\sigma_2),(\sigma_3 \ldots \sigma_L)}Ψ(a1​σ2​),(σ3​…σL​)​ of dimension (r1d×dL−2)(r_1 d \times d^{L-2})(r1​d×dL−2), to give

cσ1…σL=∑a1r1Aa1σ1Ψ(a1σ2),(σ3…σL).c_{\sigma_1 \ldots \sigma_L} = \sum_{a_1}^{r_1} A^{\sigma_1}_{a_1} \Psi_{(a_1\sigma_2), (\sigma_3 \ldots \sigma_L)} .cσ1​…σL​​=a1​∑r1​​Aa1​σ1​​Ψ(a1​σ2​),(σ3​…σL​)​.

Ψ\PsiΨ is subjected to an SVD, and we have

cσ1…σL=∑a1r1∑a2r2Aa1σ1U(a1σ2),a2Sa2,a2(V†)a2,(σ3…σL)=∑a1r1∑a2r2Aa1σ1Aa1,a2σ2Ψ(a2σ3),(σ4…σL),c_{\sigma_1 \ldots \sigma_L} = \sum_{a_1}^{r_1} \sum_{a_2}^{r_2} A^{\sigma_1}_{a_1} U_{(a_1\sigma_2),a_2} S_{a_2,a_2} (V^\dagger)_{a_2, (\sigma_3 \ldots \sigma_L)} \\ =\sum_{a_1}^{r_1} \sum_{a_2}^{r_2} A^{\sigma_1}_{a_1} A^{\sigma_2}_{a_1,a_2} \Psi_{(a_2\sigma_3), (\sigma_4 \ldots \sigma_L)} ,cσ1​…σL​​=a1​∑r1​​a2​∑r2​​Aa1​σ1​​U(a1​σ2​),a2​​Sa2​,a2​​(V†)a2​,(σ3​…σL​)​=a1​∑r1​​a2​∑r2​​Aa1​σ1​​Aa1​,a2​σ2​​Ψ(a2​σ3​),(σ4​…σL​)​,

where we have replaced UUU by a set of ddd matrices Aσ2A^{\sigma_2}Aσ2​ of dimension (r1×r2)(r_1 \times r_2)(r1​×r2​) with entries Aa1,a2σ2=U(a1σ2),a2A^{\sigma_2}_{a_1,a_2} = U_{(a_1\sigma_2),a_2}Aa1​,a2​σ2​​=U(a1​σ2​),a2​​ and multiplied SSS and V†V^\daggerV†, to be reshaped into a matrix Ψ\PsiΨ of dimension (r2d×dL−3)(r_2 d \times d ^{L-3})(r2​d×dL−3), where r2≤r1d≤d2r_2 \leq r_1 d \leq d^2r2​≤r1​d≤d2. or more compactly

cσ1…σL=Aσ1Aσ2…AσL−1AσL,c_{\sigma_1 \ldots \sigma_L} = A^{\sigma_1} A^{\sigma_2} \ldots A^{\sigma_{L-1}} A^{\sigma_L} ,cσ1​…σL​​=Aσ1​Aσ2​…AσL−1​AσL​,

where we have recognized the sums over a1a_1a1​, a2a_2a2​ and so forth as matrix multiplications. The last set of matrices AσLA^{\sigma_L}AσL​ in fact consists of column vectors. If we wish, dummy indices 1 may be introduced in the first and last AAA to turn them into matrices, too. In any case, the (arbitrary) quantum state is now represented exactly in the form of a matrix product state:

∣ψ⟩=∑σ1,…,σLAσ1Aσ2…AσL−1AσL∣σ1,…,σL⟩.\ket{\psi} = \sum_{\sigma_1,\ldots,\sigma_L} A^{\sigma_1} A^{\sigma_2} \ldots A^{\sigma_{L-1}} A^{\sigma_L} \ket{\sigma_1,\ldots,\sigma_L} .∣ψ⟩=σ1​,…,σL​∑​Aσ1​Aσ2​…AσL−1​AσL​∣σ1​,…,σL​⟩.

上面介绍的是左正则的，还有右正则以及混合正则。但这不是我们本次计算的重点，我们只想要知道系统的状态可以写成矩阵乘积的形式就可以了。

还需要强调的一点是：

The maximal dimensions of the matrices are reached when for each SVD done the number of non-zero singular values is equal to the upper bound (the lesser of the dimensions of the matrix to be decomposed). Counting reveals that the dimensions may maximally be (1×d)(1 \times d)(1×d), (d×d2)(d \times d^2)(d×d2), …,(dL/2−1×dL/2)\ldots, (d^{L/2-1} \times d^{L/2})…,(dL/2−1×dL/2), (dL/2×dL/2−1)(d^{L/2} \times d^{L/2-1})(dL/2×dL/2−1), …,(d2×d)\ldots, (d^2 \times d)…,(d2×d), (d×1)(d \times 1)(d×1), going from the first to the last site (I have assumed LLL even for simplicity here). This shows that in practical calculations it will usually be impossible to carry out this exact decomposition explicitly, as the matrix dimensions blow up exponentially.

∣ψ⟩=∑σtr(Mσ1…MσL)∣σ⟩(MPS for PBC).\ket{\psi} = \sum_{\sigma} tr (M^{\sigma_1} \ldots M^ {\sigma_L}) \ket{\sigma} \quad{\rm (MPS \ for \ PBC)}.∣ψ⟩=σ∑​tr(Mσ1​…MσL​)∣σ⟩(MPS for PBC).
